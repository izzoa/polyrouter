Add provider management (spec §2.2/§6.2/§8; invariants 5/6/8): a session-authenticated `api/providers` surface (list/create/get/update/delete + `test-connection`/`sync-models`) and an `api/models` list/filter API, tenant-scoped through the persistence port. Credentials are encrypted at rest with the shared `encryptSecret` under a new `PROVIDER_CREDENTIAL_KEY` (dev-fallback gated exactly like the auth secrets), decrypted only in-memory to build a #6 adapter, and never returned or logged — responses expose a `hasCredential` boolean, and action failures are sanitized to a fixed `{kind,status}` message plus an internally-generated `traceId` (never the adapter's raw error body or the upstream `x-request-id`, either of which a hostile endpoint could use to reflect the auth header). Every server-fetched `base_url` is SSRF-validated at write time and before each outbound action with a per-kind `GuardContext` (private/loopback/metadata rejected except the `local`+selfhosted loopback exception; `local` rejected under cloud), userinfo and query/fragment are rejected, and the normalized URL is persisted — with no closed provider allow-list. `sync-models` upserts the catalog atomically by `(provider_id, external_model_id)` via a new ownership-scoped `ModelAccessor.upsertForProvider` port method (`ON CONFLICT DO UPDATE`; no schema migration), writing ids/display-names/`last_synced_at` only (prices stay null for #8) and pruning nothing. Updates validate the effective merged `(kind, base_url)` tuple and preserve the credential unless explicitly supplied; deleting a provider cascades to its models and routing entries. Backed by unit tests (config gating, service security logic) and a real-Postgres e2e (credential encryption, SSRF rejection, cross-tenant 404, sanitized reflection, delete cascade, atomic concurrent upsert, and the default adapter wiring over a loopback stub).
